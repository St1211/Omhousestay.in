Lamayuru Monastery

The Ancient Buddhist Wonder in Moonland, Ladakh

About Lamayuru Monastery

Lamayuru Monastery, also known as Yuru Monastery, is one of the oldest and most important Buddhist monasteries in Ladakh. Perched dramatically on a steep mountain, it dates back to the 10th century and belongs to the Red Hat sect of Buddhism.

History

According to legend, Lamayuru was once a lake that dried up when Naropa, an Indian Buddhist yogi, caused the waters to recede. The monastery was built on this site and today houses around 150 monks.

Architecture

The monastery complex consists of several shrines, including the main temple dedicated to Sakyamuni Buddha. The walls are adorned with beautiful murals and thangkas (Buddhist paintings).

Festivals

The annual Yuru Kabgyat festival, held in summer, features masked dances and rituals that attract visitors from across Ladakh.

Moonland Landscape

The surrounding area is famous for its moon-like landscape of eroded hills, making it one of Ladakh's most photogenic locations.
